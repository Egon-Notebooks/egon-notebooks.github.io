- Difficult periods in life — loss, illness, uncertainty, relationship breakdown — can leave people feeling adrift and questioning their sense of purpose. While it would be wrong to suggest that pain always carries a hidden gift, many people find that, over time, hardship can become part of a story that includes growth, clarity, and a deeper understanding of what matters to them.
- One thing that many people find meaningful is reflecting on how they have coped with difficulty before. Recognising that you have navigated hard times in the past — even imperfectly — can remind you that you carry real strengths, even when those strengths feel invisible. This is not about forcing positivity, but about gently acknowledging your own resilience as a fact, rather than a feeling.
- Another approach that some people find helpful is focusing on small, concrete sources of meaning in daily life. This might be a conversation that felt connecting, a task completed, time spent in nature, or an act of kindness given or received. Research into post-traumatic growth suggests that meaning is often rebuilt gradually, through small moments rather than sudden insight. Allowing yourself to notice these moments, without pressure, can slowly shift how you experience a difficult season.
- Some people also find it valuable to explore questions of meaning with someone they trust — whether a friend, a faith community, or a counsellor. Talking through what feels lost, and what still feels important, can help to bring shape to experiences that otherwise feel formless and overwhelming.
- It is entirely normal to struggle to find meaning during hard times, and there is no set timeline for this process. Reaching out for professional support is a sign of self-awareness, not weakness, and many people find that with the right help, even the most difficult chapters become part of a larger, hopeful story.
