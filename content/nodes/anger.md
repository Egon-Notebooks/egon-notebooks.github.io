- Anger is a natural human emotion — one that every person experiences at some point in their life. It can arise in response to feeling hurt, threatened, frustrated, or treated unfairly, and in many situations it serves an important purpose, signalling that something needs to change. The difficulty comes when anger feels overwhelming, hard to control, or begins to affect relationships, work, and overall wellbeing.
- One of the most helpful things to understand about anger is that it is not the feeling itself that causes problems, but how it is expressed. Many people find that pausing before responding — even for a few slow, deliberate breaths — can create enough space to choose a calmer course of action. This is not about suppressing the emotion, but about giving yourself a moment to respond thoughtfully rather than react impulsively.
- Another strategy that some people find useful is identifying what lies beneath the anger. Anger often sits on top of other emotions, such as sadness, shame, fear, or a sense of powerlessness. Exploring those underlying feelings, perhaps through journalling or talking with someone trusted, can offer a clearer picture of what is really going on and what genuine needs are not being met.
- Physical activity is also something many people find supportive when managing strong emotions. Movement — whether that is a brisk walk, a gym session, or even gentle stretching — can help the body process the physiological charge that comes with intense feelings.
- If anger feels persistent, difficult to manage alone, or is affecting your quality of life and relationships, it is worth knowing that support is available. Talking with a trained professional can help you understand your patterns and develop strategies that work for your specific situation. Reaching out is a sign of self-awareness, not weakness — and things can genuinely change with the right support.
