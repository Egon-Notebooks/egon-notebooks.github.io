- Positive emotions like joy, curiosity, gratitude, and contentment are not just pleasant experiences — they play a meaningful role in overall mental wellbeing. While it is natural for emotional life to ebb and flow, nurturing positive emotions can help build resilience and a greater sense of connection to the world around us.
- One approach that many people find helpful is practicing small moments of intentional attention. This might look like pausing to notice something in your environment that brings a sense of calm or delight — the warmth of sunlight, the taste of a favourite drink, or a brief conversation that left you feeling seen. Psychological research suggests that regularly directing attention toward positive experiences, even briefly, can gradually shift how we relate to everyday life.
- Another strategy involves what researchers sometimes call "savoring" — the practice of consciously dwelling on a good experience rather than moving quickly past it. When something enjoyable happens, taking a few extra moments to absorb and appreciate it may help strengthen the emotional impact of that experience. This is different from forcing happiness; it is simply allowing good moments a little more space.
- It can also be meaningful to explore activities that connect with a personal sense of purpose or playfulness. For some people this might be creative expression, time in nature, movement, or shared laughter with someone they trust. These are deeply individual, and part of the process can be gently exploring what genuinely resonates rather than following a prescribed idea of what "should" bring joy.
- If positive emotions feel consistently out of reach, or if low mood has been persistent, that is worth paying attention to. Many people find that speaking with a mental health professional helps them understand what they are experiencing and explore a path forward. Emotional wellbeing is something that can shift and grow, and support is available.
