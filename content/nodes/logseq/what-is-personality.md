- Personality is the unique pattern of thoughts, feelings, and behaviours that shapes how each of us experiences and interacts with the world. It influences everything from how we respond to stress to how we connect with others — and understanding it a little better can be a meaningful step toward greater self-awareness.
- Psychologists have found that personality tends to be fairly consistent over time, though it is not fixed. Research suggests that personality is shaped by a combination of genetics, early experiences, culture, and the relationships we form throughout life. One widely used framework — sometimes called the "Big Five" — describes personality across five broad dimensions: openness to new experiences, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and emotional stability. These traits exist on a spectrum, and most people fall somewhere in the middle rather than at the extremes.
- Understanding your own personality traits can be a useful starting point for self-reflection. Many people find it helpful to notice patterns in their reactions — for example, recognising that they tend to feel drained after social events, or that they tend to approach change with caution. This kind of awareness can make it easier to identify situations that feel challenging and to think about what kinds of support or coping strategies might suit them best. It is also worth knowing that certain traits, such as how we regulate emotions, can shift gradually over time — particularly with support, new experiences, or meaningful relationships.
- Personality is not a label or a limitation. It is simply one lens for understanding yourself more deeply. If you find that certain aspects of how you think, feel, or relate to others are causing you distress or getting in the way of your daily life, speaking with a mental health professional can be a helpful and worthwhile step. Support is available, and things can change.
