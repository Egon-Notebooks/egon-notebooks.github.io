- Surprise and awe are among the most powerful emotional experiences available to us — and yet they're easy to overlook in the rhythm of daily life. These emotions arise when we encounter something that stretches beyond our ordinary expectations, whether it's a vast natural landscape, an unexpected act of kindness, or a piece of music that seems to reach somewhere deep inside. Research suggests that making room for these experiences may have a meaningful impact on emotional wellbeing.
- One reason awe in particular may be so restorative is that it gently shifts our attention away from ourselves. When we stand before something much larger than our own concerns — a starry sky, an ancient forest, a breathtaking piece of art — the mental chatter that fuels [[Understanding stress|stress]] and [[Rumination and how to interrupt it|rumination]] often quiets. Studies have found that people who regularly experience awe tend to report greater feelings of connection, life satisfaction, and even reduced inflammation linked to [[Understanding stress|stress]]. Many people find that intentionally seeking out awe-inspiring environments, even in small ways like visiting a local park or listening to music with full attention, can offer a meaningful sense of perspective.
- Surprise, too, plays a quiet role in mental health. Positive surprises — moments that break a routine or offer something unexpectedly good — can gently disrupt negative thought patterns. Some people find it helpful to introduce small, novel experiences into their week, such as trying a new route on a walk, exploring an unfamiliar genre of book, or learning something entirely new. Novelty encourages the brain to stay curious and engaged, which many people find supportive during difficult periods.
- If you're going through a hard time, it's worth knowing that emotional experiences like wonder and curiosity can be part of a broader path toward feeling better. Connecting with a mental health professional can also provide guidance and support tailored to where you are right now.
