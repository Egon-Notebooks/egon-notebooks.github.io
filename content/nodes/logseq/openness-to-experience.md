- Openness to experience is a personality trait that describes a tendency to seek out and appreciate new ideas, emotions, perspectives, and ways of being in the world. While it is often thought of as a fixed characteristic, research suggests that our relationship with novelty and curiosity can shift over time — and that gently cultivating openness may support emotional wellbeing and resilience.
- One area where openness to experience appears to be beneficial is in how people process difficult emotions. Those who approach their inner lives with curiosity rather than judgment — noticing feelings without immediately trying to push them away — often find it easier to work through emotional challenges. Many people find that simple practices like journaling, mindful reflection, or paying attention to their reactions during everyday moments can help build this quality of curious self-awareness over time.
- Openness can also play a meaningful role in how we connect with others. Being willing to genuinely consider a different viewpoint, or to stay present in an unfamiliar conversation, can strengthen relationships and reduce feelings of isolation. This does not mean abandoning your own values or comfort — it can be as small as asking someone a question you would not normally ask, or trying an activity that sits just slightly outside your usual routine.
- It is worth noting that openness looks different for everyone, and there is no single "right" level. Some people are naturally more drawn to novelty, while others find comfort in familiarity — both are valid. The intention is not to change who you are, but to leave a little room for growth and discovery where it feels meaningful to you.
- If you are finding it hard to engage with life, new experiences, or your own emotions, talking with a mental health professional can be a valuable and supportive step. Many people find that with the right support, reconnecting with a sense of curiosity and possibility is something that can be nurtured.
