- Cognitive distortions are patterns of thinking that can skew how we interpret ourselves, other people, and the world around us. They are incredibly common — most people experience them from time to time — and they often happen automatically, beneath the surface of conscious awareness. Understanding these patterns can be a meaningful first step toward thinking in ways that feel more balanced and grounded.
- One of the most frequently experienced distortions is called [[All-or-nothing thinking|all-or-nothing thinking]], sometimes described as black-and-white thinking. This involves seeing situations in extremes — believing that if something isn't perfect, it's a complete failure, or that people are either entirely trustworthy or not at all. Many people find it helpful to gently challenge this pattern by asking whether there might be a middle ground, or a more nuanced way of looking at the situation.
- Another common pattern is [[Catastrophizing|catastrophizing]] — assuming that the worst possible outcome is likely or inevitable. This can show up as a chain of "what if" thoughts that escalate quickly, making everyday challenges feel overwhelming. Grounding exercises, such as focusing on what is actually known right now versus what is only imagined, are strategies that some people find useful in interrupting this cycle.
- Mind reading is a third distortion worth recognizing: the assumption that we know what others are thinking, usually in a negative way. This can quietly damage relationships and increase feelings of [[Understanding anxiety|anxiety]] or self-doubt. Noticing when this assumption is happening — and reminding oneself that it is an assumption, not a fact — can create a little helpful distance from the thought.
- Learning to recognize these patterns doesn't mean criticizing yourself for having them. It's a skill that takes practice, and no one has to figure it out alone. Working with a mental health professional can offer personalized support for exploring and shifting thought patterns in a way that feels manageable.
