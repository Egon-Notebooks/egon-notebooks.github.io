- Everyday life can pull our attention in many directions at once — towards deadlines, worries, and the endless scroll of tasks. The body scan is a simple mindfulness practice that gently invites attention back to the present moment, using physical sensations as an anchor. It requires no special equipment or experience, and many people find it a useful way to reconnect with themselves when life feels overwhelming.
- The practice involves slowly moving awareness through different parts of the body, from the feet upward or the head downward, noticing whatever sensations are present without trying to change them. This might mean observing tension in the shoulders, warmth in the hands, or the steady rhythm of the breath. The goal is not relaxation, though that is something many people experience — it is simply curious, non-judgmental attention. Research suggests that regular body scan practice may help reduce stress and support emotional regulation over time.
- One way to begin is to set aside five to ten minutes in a quiet space, lying down or sitting comfortably. Moving slowly through each area of the body, it can help to breathe gently into any place where tension is noticed, without pressure to feel any particular way. Some people find it easier to follow a guided audio recording when starting out, which can offer a steady, calming structure to the practice.
- For people living with anxiety, chronic stress, or conditions that affect the relationship between mind and body, the body scan may offer a gentle point of entry into greater self-awareness. It is worth noting that for some individuals, sustained attention to bodily sensations can feel uncomfortable at first — this is normal, and it is always okay to pause or adjust the practice.
- Building any new habit takes time, and self-compassion is a valuable companion along the way. If you are navigating difficult feelings and would like additional support, speaking with a mental health professional can make a meaningful difference.
