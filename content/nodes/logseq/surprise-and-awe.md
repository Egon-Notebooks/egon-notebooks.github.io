- Surprise and awe are two of the most quietly powerful emotional experiences available to us. Whether it's the sudden beauty of a sunset, an unexpected act of kindness, or a piece of music that seems to reach somewhere deep inside — these moments have a way of pulling us out of our everyday thoughts and reminding us that the world is larger than our worries.
- Research into the psychology of awe suggests that these experiences can have a meaningful effect on our wellbeing. Many people find that moments of awe naturally quiet the inner voice that tends to ruminate on stress and self-criticism. This happens partly because awe shifts our attention outward — toward something bigger than ourselves — which can create a temporary but real sense of relief from the mental noise of daily life.
- Surprise, even in small doses, can also play a role in emotional health. Routine has its comforts, but gentle novelty — trying a different walking route, exploring an unfamiliar genre of music, or saying yes to an unexpected invitation — can help stimulate curiosity and engagement. Many people find that actively seeking out small moments of wonder, rather than waiting for them to arrive, becomes a meaningful practice over time. Spending time in nature, engaging with art, or simply pausing to notice something ordinary in a new way are all approaches that some people find helpful in cultivating this sense of openness.
- It's worth remembering that emotional wellbeing isn't only built through managing difficult feelings — it also grows through nurturing positive ones. If you find that wonder feels hard to access, or that life has felt flat or disconnected for a while, that experience is worth paying attention to. Speaking with a mental health professional can be a helpful step in understanding what you're going through and finding a way forward that feels right for you.
