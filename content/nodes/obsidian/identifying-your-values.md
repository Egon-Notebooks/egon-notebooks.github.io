# Identifying your values

Living in alignment with what truly matters to us can have a profound effect on our sense of purpose and wellbeing. Yet many people move through daily life without pausing to ask a simple but powerful question: what do I actually value? Taking time to identify your core values — the principles and qualities that feel most meaningful to you — can offer a useful anchor, especially during periods of stress or uncertainty.

One approach many people find helpful is reflecting on moments when they have felt most like themselves. These might be times of connection, creativity, contribution, or quiet contentment. Noticing what those experiences have in common can begin to reveal what genuinely matters to you, rather than what you feel you *should* value. It can help to write these reflections down, since seeing thoughts on paper often makes patterns easier to spot.

Another useful practice involves looking at where tension or discomfort arises in everyday life. When something feels consistently wrong or draining, it may be a signal that an important value is being overlooked or compromised. Recognizing this is not a cause for self-criticism — it is simply information, and information can lead to change. Small adjustments to how we spend our time or energy, made with our values in mind, can gradually shift how connected and grounded we feel.

It is also worth knowing that values are not fixed. They can evolve as we grow, experience loss, build relationships, or move through different life stages. Revisiting them periodically, rather than treating them as a one-time exercise, allows them to remain a living, relevant guide.

If you find this kind of self-reflection difficult to do alone, support is available. Speaking with a mental health professional can offer a gentle, structured space to explore what matters most to you and how to move towards it.
