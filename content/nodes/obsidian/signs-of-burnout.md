# Signs of burnout

Burnout is something many people experience, yet it can be surprisingly easy to miss — especially when life is busy and pushing through feels like the only option. Understanding what burnout looks and feels like is an important first step toward doing something about it.

One of the most common signs of burnout is a persistent sense of exhaustion that doesn't seem to ease, even after rest. This isn't just ordinary tiredness at the end of a long week. It can feel like a deep, bone-level fatigue that carries into mornings and weekends alike. Alongside this, many people notice a growing sense of detachment — from their work, their relationships, or activities they once enjoyed. Things that used to feel meaningful can start to feel hollow or like just another task to get through.

Another sign worth paying attention to is a change in how effectively a person feels they're functioning. This might show up as difficulty concentrating, making decisions, or completing tasks that would normally feel straightforward. Some people also notice physical signs such as frequent headaches, disrupted sleep, or a lower resistance to illness. It is worth noting that these experiences exist on a spectrum — burnout tends to develop gradually rather than appearing all at once.

Emotional signs can also be present. Increased irritability, a feeling of being stuck, or a quiet sense of dread about the day ahead are all things many people going through burnout describe.

If any of this sounds familiar, it can help to know that burnout is not a personal failing, and it does not have to be a permanent state. Many people find that recognizing what they are experiencing is itself a meaningful step forward. Reaching out to a trusted person or a mental health professional can open up new ways of understanding and managing what you are going through. Support is available, and things can change.
