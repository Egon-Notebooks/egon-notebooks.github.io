# What is personality

Personality is the unique pattern of thoughts, feelings, and behaviours that shapes how each of us experiences and interacts with the world. It influences everything from how we respond to stress, to the kinds of relationships we build, to the way we make decisions. Understanding personality — both our own and others' — can be a meaningful step toward greater self-awareness and compassion.

One helpful way to think about personality is as a collection of relatively stable traits that tend to show up consistently across different situations. Psychologists have identified broad dimensions of personality, such as how outgoing or reserved a person tends to be, how they handle uncertainty, and how they relate to others emotionally. These traits are shaped by a mix of genetics, early experiences, culture, and environment — meaning personality is not simply something we are born with, but something that develops over time.

Importantly, personality is not fixed or unchangeable. Research suggests that many people experience gradual shifts in their personality traits throughout their lives, often becoming more emotionally stable or agreeable with age. Life experiences, meaningful relationships, therapy, and personal growth can all play a role in how our personalities evolve. Some people find that exploring their personality helps them recognise patterns that may be holding them back, or strengths they had not previously appreciated.

It is also worth noting that there is no "ideal" personality type. Each person's unique combination of traits brings its own strengths and challenges. If you feel that certain aspects of your personality are causing you significant distress or difficulty in your relationships or daily life, speaking with a mental health professional can be a valuable and supportive next step. Many people find that this kind of exploration leads to greater self-understanding and a fuller sense of who they are.
