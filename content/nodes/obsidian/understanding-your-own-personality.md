# Understanding your own personality

Getting to know yourself more deeply can be one of the most rewarding journeys a person can take. Understanding your own personality — the patterns in how you think, feel, and relate to the world — can help you make sense of your experiences, strengthen your relationships, and approach challenges with greater self-compassion.

One place to start is simply paying attention to your patterns over time. Many people find it helpful to keep a journal, noting which situations tend to energize or drain them, what kinds of environments help them feel focused and calm, and how they tend to respond under pressure. Over time, these observations can reveal consistent themes that reflect your underlying personality traits. This kind of reflection is not about labeling yourself, but about gathering information that can help you understand your needs.

Another useful approach is seeking perspective from people you trust. Those close to us often notice things about our behaviour and character that we might overlook in ourselves. Asking a trusted friend or family member how they experience you — approached with genuine curiosity rather than a need for reassurance — can offer valuable insight. Structured tools such as well-researched personality frameworks can also provide a starting point for reflection, though it is worth holding any results loosely rather than treating them as fixed definitions of who you are.

It is also worth remembering that personality is not rigid. While certain core traits tend to be stable, people grow, adapt, and change throughout their lives — particularly when they engage in meaningful experiences or personal development work. Recognising this can be genuinely encouraging.

If you find that exploring your inner world brings up difficult emotions or questions, speaking with a mental health professional can provide a safe and supportive space to work through them. You do not have to navigate self-discovery alone.
