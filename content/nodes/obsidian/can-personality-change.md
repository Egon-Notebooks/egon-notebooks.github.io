# Can personality change

Personality feels like one of the most fixed things about us — the way we tend to think, feel, and relate to the world seems deeply familiar, almost like a signature. It's natural to wonder whether these patterns can genuinely shift over time, or whether we are simply wired a certain way. Research increasingly suggests that personality is more flexible than many people assume, and that meaningful change is possible across a lifetime.

Studies in personality psychology show that people do change, often gradually and sometimes quite significantly, as they move through different life stages and experiences. Traits like emotional stability, conscientiousness, and openness to new experiences tend to evolve — particularly during periods of growth, challenge, or transition. This doesn't happen overnight, but the idea that personality is permanently fixed from early adulthood is not well supported by the evidence.

There are several ways that people may support meaningful shifts in how they experience themselves and engage with the world. Therapeutic approaches such as talking therapies can help people understand long-standing patterns and gently work toward different ways of responding to situations. Developing new habits and practices over time — such as building routines that support wellbeing, practising self-reflection, or gradually stepping outside comfort zones — can also contribute to lasting change. Many people find that supportive relationships and communities play a significant role in how they grow and who they become.

It's worth holding onto the idea that struggling with certain patterns of thinking, feeling, or behaving does not mean those patterns are permanent. People seek support for a wide range of reasons, including a desire to understand themselves better or to live differently — and that is a completely valid reason to reach out. Speaking with a mental health professional can be a helpful first step for anyone who feels their patterns of thought or behaviour are getting in the way of the life they want to live.
