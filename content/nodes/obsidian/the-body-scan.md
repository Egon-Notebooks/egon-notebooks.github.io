# The body scan

Paying attention to the body can be a powerful way to support mental wellbeing. The body scan is a mindfulness-based practice that involves slowly and gently directing your attention through different parts of the body, noticing any sensations that are present without trying to change or judge them. It is used in a number of evidence-based approaches to mental health care and many people find it a helpful way to feel more grounded and present.

One of the reasons the body scan can be beneficial is that it gently shifts attention away from busy or difficult thoughts and brings awareness into the present moment. When we are stressed or anxious, we often live very much "in our heads." Taking a few minutes to notice physical sensations — such as warmth, tension, or the feeling of breathing — can help interrupt that cycle. Many people find that even a short body scan practice leaves them feeling more settled and aware.

The practice itself is straightforward to try. Lying or sitting comfortably, many people begin by bringing attention to the feet, then slowly moving upward through the legs, torso, arms, and head. The aim is simply to notice what is there — not to relax on demand or achieve a particular feeling. If the mind wanders, which is completely normal, the practice involves gently guiding attention back without self-criticism. Over time, some people find this quality of gentle, non-judgmental awareness becomes easier to access throughout daily life.

Like many wellbeing practices, the body scan is not a replacement for professional support, and it may not suit everyone. If you are finding it difficult to manage your mental health, speaking with a healthcare professional is a meaningful and worthwhile step. Support is available, and reaching out is a sign of strength.
