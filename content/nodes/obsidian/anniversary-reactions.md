# Anniversary reactions

Certain dates can carry unexpected emotional weight. An anniversary reaction is a shift in mood, thoughts, or behaviour that occurs around the time of a significant past event — such as a loss, trauma, or major life change. These reactions are a natural part of how the mind processes meaning and memory, and experiencing one does not mean something is wrong with you.

Anniversary reactions can take many forms. Some people notice a low mood, increased anxiety, or a sense of restlessness in the days or weeks surrounding a significant date, without immediately connecting it to the calendar. Others may find that memories surface more frequently, sleep becomes disrupted, or they feel more emotionally sensitive than usual. Recognising that these feelings may be linked to an anniversary can itself bring a measure of relief — it gives the experience context and meaning.

There are several approaches that many people find helpful when navigating these periods. Acknowledging the date intentionally, rather than trying to push past it, can reduce the sense of being caught off guard by difficult emotions. Some people find it meaningful to create a small ritual — lighting a candle, visiting a place that holds significance, or spending time with someone who understands. Gentle physical activity, time in nature, and writing down thoughts and feelings are also strategies that many find grounding during emotionally charged times. Reducing pressure on yourself around these dates — lowering expectations of productivity or social obligations — can also create space for the feelings to move through without becoming overwhelming.

It is worth remembering that anniversary reactions tend to ease over time, and that the intensity of a reaction is not a measure of how much progress you have or haven't made. If these periods feel particularly difficult to manage, speaking with a mental health professional can offer meaningful support. You do not have to navigate these moments alone, and help is available.
