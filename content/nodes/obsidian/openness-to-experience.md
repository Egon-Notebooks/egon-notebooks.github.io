# Openness to experience

Openness to experience is a personality trait that describes a curiosity about the world — a willingness to engage with new ideas, emotions, perspectives, and ways of being. While it varies naturally from person to person, research suggests that cultivating this quality, even in small ways, can support emotional wellbeing and psychological flexibility.

One way openness can benefit mental health is by encouraging a less rigid relationship with our own thoughts and feelings. When we approach difficult emotions with curiosity rather than judgment — asking "what is this feeling telling me?" rather than pushing it away — many people find it easier to process and move through challenging experiences. This is sometimes called an "observational stance," and it forms a core part of several evidence-based therapeutic approaches.

Engaging with creative or unfamiliar experiences is another avenue that many people find meaningful. This might look like trying a new form of creative expression, spending time in nature, exploring a different cultural perspective through reading or conversation, or simply taking a different route on a familiar walk. These actions do not need to be dramatic — small moments of genuine novelty can gently stretch our sense of what is possible, both in the world and within ourselves.

Openness also involves a willingness to be uncertain — to hold questions without needing immediate answers. For those navigating periods of stress or change, this quality can act as a buffer, helping people remain adaptable rather than overwhelmed. Some people find that mindfulness practices support this, creating a little more space between an experience and a reaction.

If you are finding it difficult to engage with life openly — perhaps because anxiety, low mood, or past experiences have narrowed your world — this is something that support can genuinely help with. Many people find that speaking with a mental health professional opens up new ways of relating to themselves and others. You do not have to figure it out alone.
