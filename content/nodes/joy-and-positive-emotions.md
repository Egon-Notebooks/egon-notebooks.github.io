- Positive emotions like joy, curiosity, warmth, and wonder are more than fleeting moments of feeling good — they play a meaningful role in overall mental wellbeing. Research suggests that experiencing positive emotions regularly can help build resilience, strengthen relationships, and support us during more difficult times. This doesn't mean life should feel happy all the time, or that difficult emotions should be pushed aside. Rather, nurturing joy when it arises can be a gentle and powerful part of caring for your mental health.
- One approach that many people find helpful is practising what psychologists sometimes call "savouring" — taking a few moments to pause and fully notice a positive experience as it happens. This might be as simple as sitting with the warmth of a cup of tea, noticing the pleasure of a conversation with someone you trust, or pausing to appreciate something beautiful in your surroundings. Over time, small habits like these may help the mind become more attuned to positive moments that might otherwise pass unnoticed.
- Another evidence-informed strategy involves doing things that align with your values or sense of meaning — not just things that feel immediately pleasurable. Activities like helping others, engaging in creative work, spending time in nature, or learning something new can generate a deeper, more sustained sense of wellbeing. Many people also find that connecting with others, even briefly, is one of the most reliable sources of positive emotion in everyday life.
- It is worth remembering that if joy feels persistently out of reach, that in itself can be a sign that some additional support might be helpful. Difficulty experiencing positive emotions is a recognised part of many mental health challenges, and it is something that can improve with the right care. Speaking with a mental health professional is a meaningful step — and one that many people find makes a real difference.
